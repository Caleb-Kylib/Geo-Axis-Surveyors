What to Do If Someone Encroaches on Your Land

Updated: April 10, 2026 Surv. Martin Kimani 11 Min Read

Quick Answer

If you suspect encroachment: 1. Do not confront with violence; 2. Hire a licensed surveyor to perform a "Boundary Re-establishment"; 3. Obtain a Survey Report as legal proof; and 4. Issue a formal notice through a lawyer. If the neighbor refuses to move, the report becomes your primary evidence in the Environment and Land Court.

In This Article:
  • Identifying the Encroachment
  • The Boundary Re-establishment Process

Finding out that a neighbor has built a wall, a shed, or even a main house on your property is a stressful experience. In Kenya, land is a sensitive issue, and handling these disputes correctly is the difference between winning in court and losing your land.

1. Identifying the Encroachment

Encroachment isn't always obvious. It could be a fence that is "creeping" by a few inches every year or a permanent structure that blocks your access. Before making accusations, you must be certain. Visual estimation is not legal proof.

2. The Boundary Re-establishment Process

This is where a licensed surveyor comes in. We use the original Survey Plan to locate the precise coordinates of your beacons. If they have been moved or removed, we re-establish them using high-precision GNSS equipment. We then provide a Survey Report that clearly shows the area of overlap.

Get Proof, Not Opinions

Our surveyors provide certified reports that are admissible in Kenyan courts as expert evidence in boundary disputes.

Request a Boundary Survey

3. Legal Steps to Reclaim Your Land

Once you have the survey report:

  1. Dialogue: Show the report to your neighbor. Often, encroachment is accidental.
  2. Mediation: If dialogue fails, involve the local Land Registrar or the Village Elders for mediation.
  3. Demand Letter: Have your lawyer send a formal letter demanding the removal of the encroachment within a specific timeframe.
  4. Court Action: File a case in the Environment and Land Court for an eviction or demolition order.

4. How to Prevent Future Encroachment

The best defense is a clear boundary. We recommend:

  • Permanent Fencing: Use stone walls or chain-link fences with concrete posts.
  • Regular Inspections: Visit your land at least once a quarter if you don't live there.
  • Beacon Maintenance: Ensure your beacons are visible and painted with a distinct color.

To avoid buying land that is already encroached, always perform a Beacon Verification before purchase.
