What to Expect During a Land Survey

February 5, 2026 Eng. John Kimani 7 Min Read

Whether you are subdividing land or settling a boundary dispute, a professional land survey is the foundation of property security. Here is what happens when our team arrives on-site.

Phase 1: Research

Before leaving the office, we gather historical survey data, registry maps, and deed plans from the Survey of Kenya to understand the legal framework of your land parcels.

Phase 2: On-Site Measurements

Our field crew uses high-precision total stations and GNSS equipment to locate existing boundary markers and take new measurements. You might see us setting up tripods and using "poles" to sight points across the land.

Phase 3: Analysis and Reporting

Back in the office, we analyze the field data against the legal records. We then prepare the final survey plan or beacon certificate for your property registration needs.
