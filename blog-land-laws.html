Understanding Kenyan Land Laws

March 1, 2026 Surv. Martin Kimani 10 Min Read

Kenyan land laws have undergone significant transformations over the last decade. Understanding these laws is essential for anyone looking to invest in property, whether for residential or commercial purposes.

Categories of Land Ownership

In Kenya, land is categorized into three main types: Public land, Community land, and Private land. Private land is held by individuals or entities and is protected under the Constitution.

Leasehold vs Freehold

Freehold gives the owner absolute ownership for an indefinite period. Leasehold, on the other hand, is land held on a lease from the government for a specific period (usually 99 years).

The Role of the Land Registry

The Land Registry is responsible for maintaining all land records in Kenya. With the introduction of the ArdhiSasa platform, many processes have been digitized to improve transparency and efficiency.
