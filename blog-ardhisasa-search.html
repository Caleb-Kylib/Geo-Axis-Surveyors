How to Conduct a Land Search in Kenya (ArdhiSasa Guide)

Updated: April 02, 2026 Surv. Martin Kimani 10 Min Read

Quick Answer

To conduct a land search, log in to ArdhiSasa.lands.go.ke, navigate to "Land Search", enter the Parcel Number (e.g., Nairobi/BlockXXX/123), and pay the KES 500 fee via M-Pesa. The results will be available for download as a PDF within 24-48 hours.

The digital transformation of the Ministry of Lands has made property verification faster and more transparent. Gone are the days of manual searches; ArdhiSasa is now the "single source of truth" for land ownership in Kenya.

1. Creating Your ArdhiSasa Account

Before you can perform a search, you must have a verified profile. You will need your National ID and a functional phone number linked to your ID. Verification can take up to 48 hours as the system cross-references your data with the Integrated Population Registration System (IPRS).

2. Step-by-Step Search Process

Once logged in, follow these steps:

  1. Go to the "Services" tab.
  2. Select "Land Search".
  3. Click on "New Application".
  4. Enter the Title Number exactly as it appears on the Title Deed.
  5. Provide the purpose of the search (e.g., Personal Verification).
  6. Authorize the KES 500 payment via the M-Pesa STK push.

Is Your Parcel Missing?

Many old titles are still being "cleaned up" in the digital migration. If your parcel doesn't show up, our team can help you with the manual-to-digital conversion process.

3. Interpreting the Results

The search result (Certificate of Official Search) will show:

  • Proprietorship Section: Name and ID of the current owner.
  • Property Section: Size of the land and nature of title (Freehold/Leasehold).
  • Encumbrances Section: This is critical. It lists any charges (loans), caveats, or court injunctions.

4. Common Issues and Fixes

If the system says "No Record Found," it might be because the land is still registered in an old manual registry that hasn't been digitized yet. In such cases, you may need to visit the local Land Registry office for a manual search or initiation of the digitization process.

A search confirms ownership on paper. To confirm the land physically, see our Guide to Beacon Verification.
